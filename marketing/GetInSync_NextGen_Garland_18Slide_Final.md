### SLIDE 6 — Portfolio Isolation & Controlled Sharing (Pitch)

**Subtitle:** When the Police department logs in, they see only their applications — CAD, records management, body cameras. No scrolling past HR or Finance. Their dashboard, their data — nothing else.

**No noise.** Every user and manager sees a dashboard populated only with the applications that matter to them. Nobody wades through hundreds of applications to find the ones that are theirs.

**Complete autonomy.** Each division operates independently — running their own assessments, managing their own contacts, tracking their own costs. Think of each Workspace as a mini-GetInSync for that division.

**Enforced at the database level.** Row Level Security means if you're not a member of a Workspace, the data doesn't exist to you. Not hidden — absent.

**Portfolios still work the way you know them.** Inside each Workspace, portfolios are how you group and report on applications.

**Four portfolio boxes on the slide:**
- Financial Services Portfolio
- Public Safety — Police Portfolio
- Human Resources Portfolio
- Information Technology Portfolio

**Workspace Groups:** IT publishes shared apps (M365, network, cybersecurity). Other divisions consume them — one record, no duplication, costs allocated once.

**Leadership views** span all portfolios for reporting — without breaking isolation.

### SLIDE 8 — Assessment Framework — TIME, PAID & Criticality (Pitch)

**Subtitle:** Ten business questions. Fourteen technical questions. Two frameworks. Criticality isn't an afterthought — it's baked into every assessment.

**TIME** (Technical Health vs Business Fit) — Tolerate, Invest, Modernize, Eliminate.
*"What should we do with this application?"*

**PAID** *(new)* (Criticality vs Tech Risk) — Plan, Address, Ignore, Delay.
*"How urgently do we need to act?"*

**Criticality is derived** — not a separate field someone fills in. It comes directly from the business assessment scores. No manual tagging, no subjective override.

**Configurable weights and thresholds** — Your organization decides what matters most. Factor weights and quadrant thresholds are adjustable at the organizational level.

## AUDIT NOTES

The following adjustments were made based on a codebase audit (April 13, 2026) that validated every claim against the deployed schema and frontend:

- **Slide 4 (Cost):** "Automated alerts" softened to "expiry dashboard with status badges." "Never" softened to "warning prompts." "YoY Trends" changed to "Multi-Year Budget Tracking."
- **Slides 8–10 (Assessment):** New slides — audit flagged that the core assessment engine was completely absent from the original deck.
- **Slide 11 (AI):** Replaced two unverifiable queries (YoY spend trend, no-business-owner) with verified queries (total IT spend by workspace, vendors with most applications).
- **Slide 13 (Tech Health):** "Automatically" softened to "AI-assisted." "$60K+" corrected to "$20K–$100K+." Technology Product categories corrected from 15 to 16.
- **Slide 15 (Security):** SSO reframed as "Enterprise OAuth." Evidence collection reframed as "one-command." Data residency reframed as "by design — US/EU on demand." Steward and Restricted flagged as Q2 2026.
- **Slide 17 (CSDM):** Replaced the original ITSM Integration slide entirely. No ITSM publish/sync code exists. CSDM data model alignment is real and deployed.
- **ServiceNow:** All references removed. Positioned as "industry-standard CSDM" and "CSDM-compliant ITSM platform."
- **Police context:** Clarified as "the Police department" on first mention to establish city-department context for APM.
- **Budget/financials "today":** Original platform has only a total cost field with no timeframe, no annual budget, no vendor rollups, no workspace allocation. Costs were estimates attached directly to applications, risking double-counting on shared technology.
