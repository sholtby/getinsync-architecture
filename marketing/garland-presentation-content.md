## SLIDE 4 — Portfolio Isolation & Controlled Sharing

Lead with the experience, not the architecture.

**Subtitle:** When Police logs in, they see Police. No noise. No scrolling past Libraries or Finance applications to find their own. Their dashboard, their data, their assessments — nothing else.

Key messages:

- **No noise** — Every user and manager sees a dashboard populated only with the applications that matter to them. Police sees CAD, RMS, and body cam systems. Finance sees payroll, budgeting, and tax applications. Nobody wades through hundreds of applications to find the ones that are theirs.
- **Complete autonomy** — Each division operates independently — running their own assessments, managing their own contacts, tracking their own costs. Think of each Workspace as a mini-GetInSync for that division. They don't need to coordinate with other divisions to get their work done.
- **Enforced at the database level** — This isn't a filter you can toggle off. Row Level Security means if you're not a member of a Workspace, the data doesn't exist to you. Not hidden — absent.
- **Portfolios still work the way you know them** — Inside each Workspace, portfolios are how you group and report on applications. That hasn't changed.

Workspace Groups — Publisher / Consumer model:

IT publishes shared applications like M365, network infrastructure, and cybersecurity tools. Other divisions consume them — they see the app, they can assess it in their context, but IT retains ownership. One record, no duplication, costs allocated once.

Leadership view:

Cross-Workspace views let leadership report across all divisions without breaking the isolation that protects each team's data.

- Financial Services Portfolio
- Public Safety — Police Portfolio
- Information Technology Portfolio

## SLIDE 5 — AI-Powered Portfolio Intelligence

*Stop building reports. Start asking questions.*

Five example queries, all cost/vendor/risk focused. Each shown as a chat-style prompt with a one-line explanation of what it queries.

Example queries:

- **"How much are we spending with Oracle across all departments?"** — Queries vendor spend across IT Services and Cost Bundles, rolls up by vendor.
- **"Which contracts expire in the next 90 days?"** — Scans contract lifecycle data, flags renewals due by date range.
- **"What's our year-over-year spend trend in Public Safety?"** — Pulls budget history by portfolio, calculates trend direction.
- **"Show me all applications with no assigned business owner"** — Identifies gaps in ownership — data quality you can act on.
- **"Which applications run on end-of-support technology?"** — Cross-references technology tags with lifecycle reference dates.

Bottom line: Every question runs against your live data — not a static export. Answers in seconds, not days.

## SLIDE 8 — Granular Access Controls

**Subtitle:** The right people see the right things — and the right people can update their own applications.

This slide is about delegation, not just permissions. The key message: instead of your team chasing application owners for assessment updates, invite them in.

Five role levels:

- **Admin** — Full control over the portfolio, users, and settings.
- **Editor** — Creates and modifies applications, assessments, and costs.
- **Steward** — This is your business owner. Invite application owners directly into the platform to assess their own applications. They update what's assigned to them — no training required, no risk of touching someone else's data.
- **Viewer** — Read-only access to everything in the portfolio.
- **Restricted** — Read-only access limited to assigned applications only. This is how Police stays curtained — Restricted users in other portfolios can't even see Police applications exist.

Key talking point:

*Instead of your team chasing hundreds of application owners for assessment updates, invite them in as Stewards. They see only their apps. They answer the questions. You get the data.*
