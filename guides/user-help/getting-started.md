# Getting Started with GetInSync

Welcome to GetInSync — the simplest way to understand your application portfolio's health, cost, and modernization plan in one place.

This guide walks you through your first session so you can start seeing value immediately.

---

## What is GetInSync?

GetInSync is an Application Portfolio Management (APM) platform. It helps you answer three questions about your IT applications:

1. **What do we have?** — A clear inventory of every application, where it runs, and who owns it.
2. **How healthy is it?** — Objective scores for business fit, technical health, and infrastructure risk.
3. **What should we do about it?** — A funded roadmap of modernization, migration, and retirement initiatives.

Think of it as a health check for your entire IT landscape.

---

## Key Concepts

Before you dive in, here are four terms you will see everywhere:

| Term | What It Means |
|------|---------------|
| **Namespace** | Your organization's account. Everything you do lives inside your namespace. |
| **Workspace** | A logical grouping of applications (e.g., "Finance Department" or "Toronto Office"). You can have multiple workspaces. |
| **Portfolio** | A logical grouping of applications within a workspace (e.g., "Finance Apps" or "Citizen Services"). Portfolios can be nested into hierarchies. |
| **Application** | A business application in your portfolio (e.g., "SAP ERP", "Salesforce CRM"). |
| **Deployment Profile** | How and where a specific application runs. The same app can have multiple profiles — for example, one running on-premises and another as SaaS. |

## Your First 5 Minutes

### 1. Select Your Workspace

Use the **scope bar** at the top of the screen to pick your workspace. This filters the entire dashboard to show only that workspace's applications.

If you manage multiple workspaces, you can switch between them anytime.

### 2. Explore the Overview Dashboard

The **Overview** tab shows your portfolio at a glance:

- **KPI cards** — Total applications, health scores, cost summary, and key metrics
- **Portfolio breakdown** — How your applications are distributed across categories
- **Quick actions** — Jump to any application or start an assessment

### 3. Browse Your Applications

Click any application name to open its detail view. You will see:

- Basic information (owner, category, status)
- Deployment profiles (where and how it runs)
- Assessment scores (if assessed)
- Integrations (what it connects to)

### 4. Run Your First Assessment

Assessments are how you score an application's business value and technical health. The results place your app on the **TIME quadrant** — telling you whether to Tolerate, Invest, Modernize, or Eliminate it.

See [How to Assess an Application](/assessment-guide) for a step-by-step walkthrough.

## Navigating the Platform

GetInSync is organized into tabs along the top:

| Tab | What It Shows |
|-----|---------------|
| **Overview** | Portfolio-wide KPIs, summaries, and quick actions |
| **App Health** | The TIME and PAID quadrant charts — your applications plotted by business fit vs. technical health |
| **Tech Health** | Infrastructure risk dashboard — end-of-life platforms, unsupported versions, and lifecycle status |
| **Roadmap** | Funded initiatives — what to fix, when, and at what cost |

### Search Everything

Press **Ctrl+K** (or **Cmd+K** on Mac) to open Global Search. Search across applications, services, contacts, technologies, and more — all from one search bar.

## Your Profile and Avatar

Your name and photo appear in the **user menu** at the top-right of the screen. GetInSync automatically displays your photo if you signed in with Google or Microsoft. If no photo is available, it shows your initials in a colored circle.

### Uploading a Profile Photo

You can upload your own photo to replace the default:

1. Click your name in the top-right corner to open the user menu
2. Select **Settings**
3. In the **Profile Photo** section, click **Upload Photo**
4. Choose an image file (JPEG, PNG, WebP, or GIF — max 2 MB)
5. Your new photo appears immediately across the platform

To remove a custom photo, click **Remove Photo** in the same section. Your avatar will fall back to your Google/Microsoft photo, Gravatar, or initials.

### Updating Your Display Name

Below the photo section, you can update your **Display Name**. Your first name is shown in the top navigation bar, and your full name appears in the user menu dropdown.
